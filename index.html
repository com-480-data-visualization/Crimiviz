Chicago Crime Explorer

Select a module to begin

This dashboard explores over two decades of crime incidents reported in Chicago, from 2001 to 2024. It is structured around three points of views : geography, time, and context. It tries to find patterns in how, when, and where crime concentrates across the city.

Map →

Geographic distribution of incidents across Chicago neighborhoods, wards, and police districts.

Trends →

Temporal patterns, seasonality analysis, time-of-day rhythms, and crime type breakdowns.

Insights →

Analytical deep-dives: COVID disruption, election cycles, and major city events correlated with crime.

Source Chicago Data Portal Crimes : 2001 to Present
Volume ~7.8 million reported incidents : updated daily
Scope All reported crimes extracted from the Chicago Police Department's CLEAR system. Excludes murders where data exists only in an additional dataset.
Choices Analysis focuses on reported incidents, not convictions. Arrest rates are included as a secondary signal. Geographic aggregation uses community areas (77 total) rather than individual addresses for privacy and legibility.
